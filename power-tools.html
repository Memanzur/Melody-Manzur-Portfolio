Client Kickoff

Before anything else, understand what they actually need. Not what they say they want, what they need.

  • What is the business? Who are the customers?
  • What problem are we solving right now?
  • What does success look like in 30/60/90 days?
  • Who is the competition? What are they doing well?
  • What has been tried before? What worked, what did not?
  • What is the budget and timeline?
  • Who is the decision maker? Who approves deliverables?

Run a full audit on whatever they have right now. Document everything before touching anything.

  • Screenshot every page (desktop + mobile)
  • Run /design-review on the live site
  • Run /qa to find bugs and broken flows
  • Run PageSpeed Insights on key pages
  • Check Google Search Console for indexing issues
  • Check analytics for traffic patterns and drop-offs
  • Review SEO basics (titles, descriptions, headings, alt text)

Know what you are up against. Find the gaps and the opportunities.

  • Identify top 3-5 direct competitors
  • Screenshot their homepages and key pages
  • Note what they do well (steal shamelessly)
  • Note what they do poorly (your opportunity)
  • Check their SEO (what keywords they rank for)
  • Check their social presence and content strategy

Agree on specific, measurable outcomes. No vague "improve the website" goals.

  • Define 3 primary goals (specific and measurable)
  • List all deliverables with deadlines
  • Set up analytics/tracking before work begins
  • Create a shared project board (GitHub Issues or Notion)
  • Schedule check-in cadence (weekly or biweekly)
  • Document the baseline metrics to measure against

Digital Marketing Toolkit

  • Unique title tag per page (50-60 characters)
  • Meta description per page (150-160 characters)
  • One H1 per page, matches the page topic
  • Heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3, no skipping)
  • Alt text on every image (descriptive, not keyword-stuffed)
  • Internal links between related pages
  • URL structure is clean and readable
  • Schema markup for business info (LocalBusiness, FAQ, etc.)
  • sitemap.xml exists and is submitted to Search Console
  • robots.txt is not blocking important pages
  • Site loads under 3 seconds (check PageSpeed Insights)
  • Mobile-friendly (passes Google mobile test)
  • HTTPS everywhere (no mixed content)
  • No broken links (404s)
  • Canonical tags on duplicate/similar pages
  • Images compressed (WebP or optimized JPEG/PNG)
  • Lazy loading on below-the-fold images
  • Core Web Vitals passing (LCP, FID, CLS)
  • Google Business Profile claimed and complete
  • NAP consistent everywhere (Name, Address, Phone)
  • Business hours, photos, and services listed
  • Encourage and respond to Google reviews
  • LocalBusiness schema on the website
  • Listed in relevant local directories
  • Google Analytics 4 installed and verified
  • Google Search Console connected
  • Goals/conversions set up (form submissions, clicks, purchases)
  • UTM parameters on all campaign links
  • Microsoft Clarity or Hotjar for heatmaps and session recordings
  • Monthly reporting template created
  • Organic traffic (week over week, month over month)
  • Top landing pages and bounce rate
  • Conversion rate by source (organic, social, direct, referral)
  • Average session duration and pages per session
  • Top performing keywords (Search Console)
  • Click-through rate on search results

Always tag campaign links so you know what is driving traffic.

utm_source: Where the traffic comes from (google, instagram, newsletter)

utm_medium: The channel type (cpc, social, email)

utm_campaign: The specific campaign name (spring-sale, launch-2026)

utm_content: Which link or ad variant (header-cta, footer-link)

  • Define 3-5 content pillars (topics you always post about)
  • Mix content types: educate (40%), entertain (30%), sell (20%), connect (10%)
  • Post consistently (3-5x/week minimum)
  • Engage with comments within 1 hour of posting
  • Repurpose content across platforms (blog to carousel to reel)
  • Use trending audio/formats but stay on brand

  • Include location-based hashtags for local businesses
  • Create a branded hashtag for the client

  • Preview text set (do not let it auto-fill)

Email 1 (immediately): Welcome + deliver what they signed up for. Set expectations for what is coming.

Email 2 (day 2): Your story. Why you do what you do. Build connection.

Email 3 (day 4): Biggest value bomb. Your best tip, guide, or resource. Prove your expertise.

Email 4 (day 7): Soft sell. Here is how I can help, here is what it looks like. Include testimonial or case study.

The classic. Works for landing pages, emails, ads.

Attention: Hook them with a bold claim or question.

Interest: Explain the problem they have.

Desire: Show how life is better with your solution.

Action: Tell them exactly what to do next.

Best for short-form copy. Emails, social captions, ads.

Problem: Name the pain. Be specific.

Agitate: Twist the knife. What happens if they do nothing?

Solution: Here is the fix. Make it feel easy.

Great for transformation stories and testimonials.

Before: What life/business looks like now (the struggle).

After: What it looks like with the solution (the dream).

Bridge: How to get from before to after (your product/service).

  • Use action verbs (Get, Start, Download, Book, Join)
  • Be specific ("Get My Free Guide" not "Submit")
  • One CTA per section, one primary CTA per page
  • Create urgency without being sleazy (limited spots, deadline)
  • Button text should finish "I want to..." (I want to Get Started)
  • Reduce friction with words like "free," "instant," "no credit card"

Question: "Still struggling with [problem]?"

How-to: "How to [achieve result] in [timeframe]"

Number: "7 [things] that [benefit]"

Curiosity gap: "The [thing] most [people] get wrong about [topic]"

Personal: "[Name], I noticed something about your [thing]"

Urgency: "[Time left] to [get benefit]"

Diff analysis that catches bugs passing CI but breaking in production. Checks for SQL safety, trust boundary issues.

Build a complete design system from scratch. Typography, color, spacing, layout. Creates a DESIGN.md file as the source of truth.

Superpowers is an agentic skills framework by Jesse Vincent at Prime Radiant. Works across Claude Code, OpenAI Codex, Cursor, Gemini CLI, and GitHub Copilot. Your agent steps back, asks what you are really trying to do, teases a spec out of the conversation, creates an implementation plan, then launches subagents to work through each task with two-stage review.

                                
Enforces true red/green TDD. Write failing test, watch it fail, write minimal code to pass, commit. Combined with YAGNI and DRY. Claude can work autonomously for hours without deviating from the plan.
